Overview

Linked Issues Progress is a Jira Cloud app built on Atlassian. It shows a readiness snapshot on an issue by rolling up the statuses of its linked and child items and rendering a simple progress bar and milestone tracker. This page explains what the app processes and how we protect your data.

Data we process

  • Within Jira (customer content & metadata, read-only): current issue key; linked issue keys; minimal fields needed to compute progress (e.g., status and statusCategory of linked issues); link type/direction metadata and project/issuetype identifiers required to render UI. We display aggregated counts and percentages in the panel.
  • Configuration data (stored in Atlassian): your admin selections for which link types/directions to include in roll-ups and other simple UI preferences.
  • Operational/diagnostic data: invocation and error logs provided by Atlassian’s platform (visible to the app owner in Atlassian’s developer console). We do not export these logs outside Atlassian systems.

What we don’t do

  • No separate user accounts or profiles.
  • No transfer of Jira customer content to non-Atlassian services.
  • No third-party analytics or advertising in the app UI.
  • No app-managed database for customer content; we do not persist issue data outside Atlassian.

If outbound integrations are added in the future, we will update this policy and the app’s permissions/CSP to list the exact domains.

Purposes of processing

  • Compute and display roll-up progress for the current issue based on linked items’ status categories.
  • Read minimal Jira metadata to populate selectors and apply your configuration.
  • Troubleshoot operational issues using Atlassian-hosted logs.

Storage & retention

  • Customer content: not stored by us outside Atlassian. The app reads Jira data on demand and renders results; aggregated values are not persisted.
  • Configuration: stored in Atlassian storage:app for as long as the app is installed or until an admin resets settings.
  • Operational logs: retained within Atlassian’s platform per Atlassian defaults. We do not export these logs.

Sub-processors & data location

The app runs entirely on Atlassian Atlassian. We do not use additional sub-processors for customer content. Data is processed within Atlassian’s infrastructure and regions applicable to your Jira Cloud tenancy and data residency settings. If this changes, we will list sub-processors here and update this policy.

Security

  • We request only minimal scopes (e.g., read:jira-work, storage:app). Storage is used to persist basic app settings
  • Access is authorized by Atlassian OAuth and enforced by Atlassian runtime isolation and Content Security Policy (CSP).
  • Any future outbound connections will be explicitly declared in egress permissions and CSP allow-lists.

Your choices & rights

  • You can uninstall the app at any time in Jira → Apps → Manage apps.
  • Because we do not store Jira customer content outside Atlassian, uninstalling ends our processing of that data.
  • For privacy requests (e.g., access or deletion of correspondence/config records), contact info@shoshins.com.

Children’s data

The app is intended for business use in Jira and is not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under applicable age thresholds.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy; updates will be posted here with a new effective date.
