Overview

Issue Links is a Jira Cloud app built on Atlassian that lets users create and link issues directly from an in-issue panel. This policy explains what the app processes and why.

Data we process

  • Within Jira (customer content & metadata): the current issue key, project and issue-type metadata, available link types, and the summary you enter for the new issue.
  • Operational/diagnostic data: invocation and error logs generated by Atlassian’s platform (visible to the app owner in the Atlassian Developer Console). We do not export these logs outside Atlassian systems.

What we don’t do

  • No separate user accounts.
  • No transfer of customer content to non-Atlassian services.
  • No third-party analytics or advertising in the app UI.
  • No app-managed database for customer content; no persistence outside Atlassian.

If we add outbound integrations in the future, we will update this page and the app’s permissions/CSP to list the exact domains.

Purposes of processing

  • Create a new issue and link it to the current issue according to your selection.
  • Read minimal Jira metadata to populate selectors (issue types, link types).
  • Troubleshoot operational problems using Atlassian-hosted logs.

Roles & legal basis

  • Controller vs processor: For Jira customer content, we act as a processor to your organization (the Jira customer). For direct communications you send us (e.g., support email), we act as a controller.
  • Legal basis (EU/UK GDPR): performance of contract and/or legitimate interests to provide and support the app.

Storage & retention

  • Customer content: not stored by us outside Atlassian. The app performs actions via Jira APIs without persisting customer content on our infrastructure.
  • Operational logs: retained within Atlassian’s platform per Atlassian defaults. We do not export these logs.

Sub-processors & data location

The app runs on Atlassian’s platform. We do not use additional sub-processors for customer content. If this changes, we will list them here and update our policy.

Security

  • Only the minimum Atlassian scopes are requested (e.g., read:jira-work, write:jira-work).
  • Access is authorized by Atlassian OAuth and enforced by runtime isolation and Content Security Policy (CSP).
  • Any future outbound connections will be explicitly declared in egress permissions and CSP allow-lists.

Your choices & rights

  • You can uninstall the app at any time in Jira → Apps → Manage apps.
  • Because we do not store customer content outside Atlassian, uninstalling ends our processing of that data.
  • For privacy requests (e.g., access or deletion of correspondence), contact us at info@shoshins.com.

Children’s data

The app is intended for business use in Jira and is not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under applicable age thresholds.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy. We will post updates on this page with a new effective date.
